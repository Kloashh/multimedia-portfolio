Rotoscope Animation

Introducing Rotoscoping and what it is

Rotoscoping is a frame-by-frame animation technique where each frame of a video is manually traced or redrawn. This labor-intensive process creates a unique "hand-drawn" effect while maintaining the precise movement and details of the original video reference.

The Rotoscoping Process

1

Choosing and downloading a video reference

The first step involves selecting and preparing the video reference. This includes choosing the right footage and adjusting frame rates for optimal rotoscoping.

2

Separating the video into individual frames

The video is split into frames using FFMPEG

3

Rotoscoping and drawing over the subject

Using Krita, the subject is traced and drawn over for that "just dance looking effect"

4

Stringing the frames together back into video

After drawing over the subject, the frames are exported as mp4

5

Final video edits

The original video is added for reference and edits are made to fit the project requirements
