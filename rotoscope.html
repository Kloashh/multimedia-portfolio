Rotoscope Animation

Introducing Rotoscoping and what it is

Rotoscoping is a frame-by-frame animation technique where each frame of a video is manually traced or redrawn. This labor-intensive process creates a unique "hand-drawn" effect while maintaining the precise movement and details of the original video reference.

The Rotoscoping Process

1

Find a video to rotoscope and import it into the software

Begin by selecting video footage with clear movement and distinct features for easier tracing. Open Adobe After Effects, import the video, and place it on the timeline or canvas.

2

Create a new layer for tracing

Add a new layer above the video and lower the opacity of the original footage so your tracing lines are easier to see.

3

Trace the subject frame by frame

Carefully trace the subject in each frame using clean lines. Focus on major shapes first, then add smaller details as the animation progresses.

4

Refine and continue the animation

Move frame by frame, adjusting your drawings slightly to match the movement. Keep proportions and line style consistent throughout the sequence.

5

Export the final rotoscoped video

Once all frames are completed, hide the original footage and review your work. Export or render the sequence into a final video file.

6

Final touches

Edit the rotoscoped video, add backgrounds, text, and any other necessary finishing touches to complete the project.
